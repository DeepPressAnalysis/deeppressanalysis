THESIS 1: DECONSTRUCTION OF FREEDOM OF NAVIGATION AND PRIVATIZATION OF MARITIME SECURITY

Behind the superficial information noise consisting of disparate crises—from the resumption of hostilities in the Middle East to technological perturbations on Wall Street—hides a fundamental reconstruction of the principles of global governance. States are losing their monopoly on power projection, logistical routes are turning into tools for geo-economic blackmail, and capital investments in artificial intelligence (AI) are destroying established business models. The era of conventional alliances is giving way to transactional cynicism and asymmetrical infrastructure wars.

Cause: The traditional dominance of Western navies has proven structurally untenable against cheap asymmetrical threats. Ukraine, using maritime drones (such as Sargan-3000 and Sea Baby), inflicted unprecedented damage on the Russian Black Sea Fleet and logistics, striking over 116 vessels (including tankers and cargo ships) in nine days in the Sea of Azov and destroying the FSB patrol ship Izumrud in Gelendzhik. This experience was instantly scaled in the Middle East. Following the collapse of the June ceasefire agreement, Iran paralyzed the Strait of Hormuz with missile strikes on commercial tankers (including the UAE vessels Mombasa and Al Bahiyah), making passage impossible without political guarantees. In response, the US administration attempted to monetize the crisis by proposing a 20% tariff on commercial vessels for military escorts, before replacing this demand with promises of Arab investments in the US economy and a return to a strict blockade of Iranian ports.

Consequence: The global system of free maritime trade, which has underpinned the global economy since 1945, has effectively ceased to exist. Maritime security has become a commercial service dependent on transactional deals (investments in exchange for patrols). In response, Gulf states (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Iraq) are accelerating the construction of overland pipelines to redirect up to 60–75% of oil exports bypassing the Strait of Hormuz by 2028. The geopolitical weight of transit hubs is declining in favor of overland continental infrastructure, permanently embedding a risk premium into energy prices.

THESIS 2: THE FINAL ECONOMIC VASSALIZATION OF RUSSIA

Cause: The four-year war of attrition in Ukraine and total isolation from Western markets have stripped the Russian Federation of political and economic sovereignty, driving it into critical dependence on China. V. Putin's visit to Beijing in May 2026 locked in a new status quo: Chairman Xi Jinping acts de facto as a manager, not a partner. A telling example is China's refusal to sign the Power of Siberia 2 gas pipeline agreement until Russia agrees to subsidize export prices to match domestic Russian levels. Concurrently, China is using the Russian market to dump excess industrial capacity: the aggressive dumping of Chinese cars and machinery is destroying the remnants of Russian sovereign manufacturing, sparking panic even among Kremlin-aligned elites (including Rostec head S. Chemezov). Meanwhile, on the front lines, Russia continues to suffer losses from regular attacks, despite conducting brutal bombardments of Ukrainian cities, such as the KAB guided bomb strikes on Kharkiv on July 15, 2026, and attacks on Kramatorsk.

Consequence: The Russian Federation has transformed into a Chinese resource colony, devoid of opportunities for technological development. Trade turnover with the PRC has reached 40% of the total volume, and export revenues depend on Beijing by a third. China blocks Russia's access to alternative financial institutions (such as the BRICS Bank or the Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank), maintaining a monopoly on lending. This structural shift means any hopes by Western strategists for Moscow's diplomatic distancing from Beijing are untenable—economic integration has passed the point of no return. At the same time, Beijing strictly rations its assistance, refusing to transfer technologies that Russia could resell to North Korea, to avoid provoking a direct confrontation with the US and Japan in the Asia-Pacific region.

THESIS 3: THE PHYSICAL LIMIT OF THE AI BUBBLE AND CANNIBALIZATION OF THE TECH SECTOR

Cause: The race for dominance in generative AI has collided with the harsh physical limitations of the material world. Hyper-scaling neural networks requires colossal volumes of electricity, water for cooling systems, and land for data centers. The capital expenditures of corporations are reaching trillions, leading to a massive reallocation of corporate IT budgets: companies are abandoning traditional software purchases in favor of AI infrastructure investments. This triggered a record 25% single-day collapse in IBM shares due to a lack of demand for its flagship z17 mainframe and drawdowns for other software giants (Salesforce, Workday, Adobe). Simultaneously, the financial sector is extracting super-profits from this boom: the largest US banks (JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Bank of America) recorded a 39% profit surge, parasitizing on underwriting fees for tech IPOs (including SpaceX) and the M&A boom.

Consequence: A fundamental redistribution of wealth is underway from the creators of traditional digital products to the owners of physical infrastructure (rentiers). Farmers in Pennsylvania are selling land for data centers for tens of millions of dollars. However, this aggressive resource consumption is meeting state resistance: New York became the first state to freeze environmental permits for large data centers (over 50 MW) for one year. Legislative barriers and energy deficits will inevitably slow the expansion of the AI industry, cutting off smaller players from infrastructure and cementing the monopoly of a few tech giants capable of building autonomous quantum supercomputers (like the state-funded $1 billion Anderon project).

THESIS 4: COLLAPSE OF THE IMMIGRATION CONSENSUS AND PARALYSIS OF THE LAW ENFORCEMENT SYSTEM

Cause: Aggressive rhetoric and quotas for arresting illegal immigrants led to the radicalization of grassroots Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) officers. The killings of Mexican and Colombian citizens during routine traffic stops in Texas and Maine provoked an international scandal and threats of lawsuits from Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum. Under pressure from the public and diplomats, ICE leadership was forced to issue a covert directive temporarily banning detentions during moving traffic stops.

Consequence: The internal immigration control system has failed at the operational level. The state finds itself caught between political promises of mass deportations and a legal/diplomatic reality where harsh methods incur unacceptable costs. Abandoning a basic patrolling tool signifies a decline in ICE's effectiveness and demoralization of the enforcement apparatus. At the same time, the destabilization of the shadow labor market (where migrants are afraid to go to work) deals a hidden blow to the agricultural complex and service sector, which will inevitably be reflected in rising inflation across basic consumer segments.

THESIS 5: THE CONSPIRACY THEORY INDUSTRY AS A TOOL FOR POLITICAL MOBILIZATION

The global information space is overloaded with constructed crises and semantic substitutions. Interested influence groups purposefully shift public attention away from systemic failures of state governance onto emotionally charged conflicts, using the media as a tool to paralyze critical thinking.

Cause: The sudden death of influential Senator Lindsey Graham (due to a medically confirmed aortic dissection) was immediately utilized by political radicals to construct an alternative reality. Figures like Laura Loomer and George Santos, with the tacit support of FBI Director Kash Patel, launched viral narratives claiming the Senator was poisoned by foreign agents (pointing to Iran or Russia due to his visit to Kyiv). Concurrently, on Russian state television, propagandists (e.g., V. Solovyov) pushed a mirror narrative alleging that Graham was "liquidated" by Ukrainian authorities over financial disputes.

Consequence: Objective reality and forensic medical expertise are losing their weight in political discourse. A self-sustaining media ecosystem is forming, in which any natural death or crisis is converted into proof of a "deep state" or external enemy. This narrative serves the interests of radical factions, allowing them to harvest political dividends and donations while ignoring the actual vulnerabilities in national security. Politics is entirely divorcing from factology, turning into a battle of paranoid scenarios.

THESIS 8: CRITICAL DEPLETION OF THE US STRATEGIC PETROLEUM RESERVE AS A TRIGGER FOR AN UPCOMING SHOCK

Critical zones of silence reveal fundamental vulnerabilities in the global architecture, which are disadvantageous for both governments and corporations to discuss publicly, as acknowledging them would require revising basic economic and political strategies.

Cause: The direct link between aggressive US policy in the Middle East and the state of internal strategic reserves is being suppressed. To smooth out price shocks from the escalation in the Strait of Hormuz and keep domestic gas prices in check ahead of political election cycles, the administration sold off the Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR), dropping its volume to the lowest level since 1983.

Consequence: The United States has lost its main macroeconomic shock absorber. In the event of a real, physical closure of the Strait of Hormuz by Iran (through large-scale mining or sinking tankers), Washington will have no leverage left to intervene in the commodities market. This means the next spike in tensions is guaranteed to translate into double-digit consumer inflation within the US. Market attention is inflated around the tech boom, while basic commodity security sits at a critically low level.

THESIS 9: HIDDEN SYSTEMIC RISK IN THE INTEGRATION OF AI INTO THE FINANCIAL SECTOR ('SILENT COVER')

Cause: Amid the euphoria over the super-profits of investment banks deploying AI algorithms for trading and data processing, the warnings of the insurance market are being systemically ignored. Over 90% of the liability for the actions of autonomous AI agents falls into the zone of "silent cover"—meaning the risks are not legally assessed and are excluded from traditional insurance policies.

Consequence: Financial markets are accumulating colossal technical debt in the form of uncontrolled risks. An algorithm failure, a massive system hallucination, or a coordinated cyberattack exploiting AI vulnerabilities could cause direct damages exceeding $100 billion, with cascading macroeconomic effects measured in the trillions. This threatens to trigger a liquidity paralysis comparable to the market collapse after the 9/11 attacks, as insurance companies will simply void coverage, leaving banks alone with their losses. The problem is underestimated due to the lobbying of tech corporations seeking to absolve themselves of any responsibility for their models' behavior.

THESIS 10: DEGRADATION OF BASIC SANITARY AND EPIDEMIOLOGICAL INFRASTRUCTURE

Cause: The information sphere is dominated by discussions of high technology, while the basic US healthcare system is undergoing a hidden collapse. An outbreak of cyclosporiasis (an intestinal parasite that infected over 5,000 people, potentially linked to fresh produce in fast-food chains like Taco Bell) demonstrated the inability of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) to promptly track threats. The root cause lies in the defunding of the FoodNet program, personnel shortages at the state level, and outdated IT systems lacking rapid DNA fingerprinting tools. Simultaneously, alpha-gal syndrome (a meat allergy caused by tick bites) is spreading, affecting up to 25% of the population in certain Southern states—a condition that 42% of doctors are unaware of.

Consequence: The collapse of grassroots monitoring systems means the state cannot contain even food poisonings, let alone new pandemics. Considering the parallel crisis of trust among migrants (who work in the agricultural sector) terrified to seek medical help due to ICE raids, an ideal environment is forming for the uncontrolled spread of infections through food supply chains. The threat to national security comes not only from foreign armies but from the rot within domestic sanitary institutions.

THESIS 11: THE LOST OPPORTUNITY TO DEPOLITICIZE THE US DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE

Many 2026 crises were not fatally predetermined. Their escalation was the result of conscious (and often erroneous) political decisions dictated by short-term electoral gains and corporate lobbying. An analysis of these diverging paths reveals where the system missed chances for stabilization.

Cause: President Trump gambled on appointing loyalist Todd Blanche (his former personal attorney) to the post of Attorney General. Blanche was compromised by attempts to protect a questionable Trump deal with the IRS, which envisioned creating a $10 billion fund to compensate "victims of political persecution" (with potential payouts to participants in the January 6 Capitol riot), as well as scandals involving the redaction of names in the Epstein files. The alternative branching point would have been the appointment of a neutral, technocratic career prosecutor, which could have eased societal tensions.

Consequence: The choice in favor of a loyalist closed the window for institutional armistice. The US Department of Justice is definitively perceived as a punitive instrument of the ruling party (accelerating cases against political opponents like J. Comey and J. Brennan). This decision has narrowed the field of choices: any future administration is now doomed to use the law enforcement system to settle scores. An external constraint has become the total polarization of the Senate, where even moderate Republicans cannot resist the pressure of the executive branch, leading to the degradation of the rule of law.

Cause: The US administration's threats to withdraw from NATO and shift costs onto allies created a historic window of opportunity for the consolidation of an independent military-industrial complex in the European Union. French President E. Macron attempted to lead this process, declaring Europe's "strategic awakening" at the Bastille Day parade. However, ambitious programs (such as the creation of a unified European stealth fighter) collapsed not due to a lack of funds, but because of infighting among national corporations (specifically, French Dassault's claims to sole leadership, which outraged Germany).

Consequence: A crucial geopolitical divergence point was squandered. Corporate egoism narrowed the field of possibilities: Europe missed the chance to become an independent, third pole of power. As a result, European states are forced to purchase American weaponry and align with US foreign policy in exchange for a fragmented nuclear umbrella, while simultaneously trying not to anger China, which controls the rare earth metals markets. The weakness of European elites has cemented their dependence on Washington and Beijing for decades to come.
