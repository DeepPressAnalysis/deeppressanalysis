▶
The 2026 IPO boom is driven less by capital needs and more by early investors (PE/VC) cashing out at market peaks. Mechanics shift toward marketing packaging, often sacrificing fundamental valuation. Retail investors are sold "growth stories" while institutionals lock in profits. Risks of inflated valuations create bubbles, especially in tech. Rating methodology could become a standard, filtering poor issuers at the pre-marketing stage, signaling the maturation of the Indian stock market toward Western transparency standards.
