Global Macro & Geopolitics

Energy • Middle East • Supply Chain • Cyber Warfare
[What changed]: Iran and the IRGC are threatening to close the strait and have launched missile strikes on U.S. bases in Qatar and Iraq in response to massive bombings.

[Why it matters]: The strait controls 20% of the global oil and LNG supply; a physical closure cannot be offset by financial instruments.

[Risk amplified]: Global energy shock, supply chain breakdown, and a severe inflation spike.
[What changed]: Oil futures plummeted 12% following a deleted tweet by the U.S. Energy Secretary and market expectations of a political end to the war.

[Why it matters]: Markets are ignoring the real physical bottleneck (Hormuz) and are overpricing political noise, creating a massive pricing anomaly.

[Risk amplified]: Sudden and severe asset repricing (margin calls) for algorithmic and commodity traders upon the convergence of futures with spot prices.
[What changed]: The confirmation of the new NSA/Cybercom head, General Radd, amidst the successful deployment of cyberattacks (power grid shutdowns) for direct regime change in Venezuela.

[Why it matters]: Cyber weapons are being officially legitimized as standard "hard power" tools in interstate conflicts.

[Risk amplified]: Asymmetric retaliatory cyberattacks targeting critical infrastructure (energy, finance, telecommunications) by antagonistic states.

Technology & Aviation

Artificial Intelligence • Tech Bubble • Aerospace Logistics
[What changed]: OpenAI is preparing for an IPO at a $730 billion valuation, while simultaneously testing ad integrations in its free products due to an unsustainable cash burn rate.

[Why it matters]: This demonstrates the exhaustion of the venture capital-funded growth model without a clear and viable B2C monetization strategy.

[Risk amplified]: The bursting of the Tech Bubble and a massive downward repricing of artificial intelligence assets.
[What changed]: Boeing is delaying 737 MAX deliveries due to new electrical wiring issues; British Airways is mass-canceling flights to the Middle East.

[Why it matters]: This represents a synchronous hit to both supply (lack of available aircraft) and demand/routing (airspace closures) within the aviation industry.

[Risk amplified]: Decreased corporate mobility, soaring freight costs, and ballooning capital expenditures (CAPEX) for airlines.
