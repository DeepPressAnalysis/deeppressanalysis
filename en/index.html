FINANCIAL TIMES

Ukraine, Nvidia, ICE, Oil, Trump & Tech Elites.
1

US gives Zelenskyy 'days' to respond to peace deal demanding loss of territory

▶
The Trump administration has shifted to "ultimatum diplomacy," demanding Kyiv's immediate agreement to freeze the conflict along current front lines in exchange for "unspecified security guarantees." Washington aims to close the "Ukraine case" before Christmas to pivot resources toward confronting China. For Ukraine, this creates an existential risk of internal destabilization; for Europe, it threatens fractured unity as the deal is pushed without full EU participation. Markets read this as a likely short-term sanctions relief signal, but long-term escalation risks remain high due to the lack of hard guarantees.
2

China plans limited access to Nvidia's H200 chips despite Trump approval

▶
A paradoxical twist: Beijing is imposing internal restrictions on purchasing advanced US chips, despite Trump granting export permissions. Chinese leadership fears technological dependency and potential sabotage of their domestic import-substitution program. This is a signal to long-term investors: "Technological sovereignty" has become more critical to the PRC than immediate economic utility. Nvidia risks losing Chinese market share not due to US sanctions, but due to Beijing's protectionism, hitting revenue forecasts.
3

ICE agents use vast data sets

▶
US Homeland Security is pivoting from counter-terrorism and drug enforcement to mass deportation, leveraging military-grade surveillance tools (facial recognition, license plate scanning). This sets a precedent for using commercial citizen data for state repressive measures. For the IT sector, it's a double-edged sword: short-term government contracts for software vendors (Palantir, Clearview AI) versus long-term reputational and regulatory risks.
4

'Super glut' to dent crude oil prices in 2026

▶
Major trader Trafigura forecasts a crash in oil prices due to a combination of slowing demand in China (EV adoption) and surging output in the US and Latin America. The market faces a structural surplus that even OPEC+ cuts cannot offset. This is a bearish signal for commodity currencies and oil major stocks, but a positive deflationary factor for importing nations.
5

Trump backs the broligarchs over his base

▶
Analysis of Trump's populist transformation: the alliance with Silicon Valley techno-libertarians (Musk, Sacks) is taking precedence over "Rust Belt" interests. Tech elites are receiving a blank check for deregulation (especially in AI and crypto), while the economic struggles of the working class are sidelined. This creates a risk of social explosion within the Republican base and heralds an era of corporatocracy.

HARVARD BUSINESS REVIEW

Transformation, Gen AI Pilots, Negotiation, Marketing, Leadership.
1

Get Off the Transformation Treadmill

▶
A critique of "permanent revolution" in business. Endless restructuring dilutes focus and destroys value. The article proposes an "adaptive ecosystem" model—continuous micro-adjustments instead of shock reforms. Investors will increasingly demand stability and operational efficiency rather than loud proclamations of a "new era."
2

A Systematic Approach to Experimenting with Gen AI

▶
AI adoption is stalling due to chaotic pilot programs. The article suggests rigorous organizational experiments (A/B testing). Key insight: AI is not just an IT project, but a workflow redesign project. Companies that fail to restructure processes to accommodate AI (rather than just buying subscriptions) will lose competitiveness.
3

Why Big Companies Struggle to Negotiate Great Deals

▶
The problem with corporations isn't a lack of negotiation skills, but structural traps: rigid mandates and bureaucracy kill flexibility. The proposal is to transform the negotiator's role into a "problem solver" empowered to make creative compromises. For business, this means decentralizing decision-making to avoid losing out to faster, more agile competitors.
4

Marketing at the Speed of Culture

▶
Traditional ad cycles are too slow for the modern world. "Fastvertising" implies instant reaction to cultural events and memes. This requires restructuring marketing departments to resemble newsrooms. The risk is losing brand identity; the reward is high emotional engagement. It marks a shift from brand strategy to brand reaction.
5

Leading After the Founder

▶
An analysis of the risks in transitioning power from a founder to a hired manager. Success depends on whether the founder can sponsor the new CEO's legitimacy without meddling in operations. For investors, this is a key checklist for assessing risk in companies undergoing generational shifts.

THE NEW YORKER

LatAm Policy, Emigration, Finance Culture, History, Urban Data.
1

Dangerous Waters

▶
US foreign policy in Latin America is becoming chaotic: the administration uses the war on drugs as a pretext for military operations that violate international law. Pardoning a convicted Honduran ex-president for political loyalty undermines trust in the US justice system. Aggressive rhetoric against Venezuela without a clear strategy raises the risk of regional conflict and asset volatility.
2

How to Leave the U.S.A.

▶
For the first time, there is a massive demand for emigration from the US driven by political and safety concerns, not economics. The American middle class is seeking asylum in Europe (e.g., via Dutch friendship treaties), fearing the erosion of democratic institutions. This is a symptom of a deep institutional crisis: the "magnet nation" is becoming a donor nation, threatening a long-term brain drain and capital flight.
3

Trading Places

▶
An analysis of the TV show "Industry" as a mirror of modern financial culture. The show depicts high finance through the eyes of the system's "cogs," for whom amorality is a survival condition. Its popularity reflects a cynical acceptance that social elevators are broken, and success depends on the ability to simulate competence. A cultural marker of Gen Z's disillusionment with capitalist meritocracy.
4

Ancient Mediterranean Incarceration

▶
Historical analysis reveals the continuity of isolation and institutional cruelty over millennia, debunking the myth that prisons are a modern invention. This questions the efficacy of current reforms: if the prison is an ancient archetype of suppression, cosmetic changes won't solve the problem. Essential for understanding the resilience of state repressive mechanisms.
5

A Data Love Letter to the Subway

▶
A visualization project of NYC subway data reveals the hidden logic of urban infrastructure. Turning the chaos of transit flows into ordered data is key to managing future megacities. For urbanists and PropTech investors, this is a signal: asset value is now defined not just by physical location, but by its digital footprint and data quality.

NEW SCIENTIST

War on Science, China Climate, Space Risks, AI Law, GLP-1.
1

The US war on science

▶
Massive budget cuts have been announced for key US scientific agencies (NIH, NASA, EPA) under the guise of "efficiency." This is a direct strike on fundamental science, threatening a brain drain and slowing innovation. For the private sector, this opens outsourcing opportunities but creates risks regarding the lack of government backing for high-risk research.
2

A turning point? China's emissions may have finally peaked

▶
Data indicates China may have passed its carbon emissions peak ahead of schedule, thanks to aggressive renewable energy deployment. This fundamentally shifts global climate models and increases pressure on Western economies. For markets, this confirms that "green tech" for the PRC is an industrial strategy for 21st-century energy dominance.
3

Damage to a Russian launch site... ISS at risk

▶
An accident at Baikonur forces NASA into a tough choice: take full responsibility for the ISS or deorbit the station early. This accelerates the fragmentation of space and the end of the era of international orbital cooperation. The situation will spur the development of private space stations and intensify competition for lunar programs.
4

AI vs copyright law

▶
The wave of lawsuits against AI developers is moving into a phase of strategic settlements. The market is forming a new data licensing model, realizing that banning neural network training is impossible. This will lead to AI market consolidation around giants capable of paying for legal content, leaving startups in a legal gray zone.
5

GLP-1s' true potential

▶
Weight-loss drugs are showing efficacy in treating a wide range of conditions, from addiction to cardiovascular disease. This heralds a tectonic shift in healthcare: pharmacology-based preventative medicine could lower chronic disease costs, making pharma giants the beneficiaries of the longevity market.

The hard luxury market is betting on "investment value." Consumers are seeking assets that hold value (rare wine, collectible bags). Sustained demand confirms a K-shaped economic recovery, where capital continues to concentrate among the wealthy.
5

The event serves as an institution for reproducing the global elite. A mix of European aristocracy and Asian business dynasties on one dance floor maps out future global capital alliances. Old money legitimizes new money, forming a unified transnational ruling class.
