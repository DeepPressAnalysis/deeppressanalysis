04

SHADOW LIQUIDITY AND THE SPV TRAP

The "shadow IPO" and SPV market is a classic derivative pyramid trading fictitious liquidity. Retail capital is funneled into multi-tiered funds where middlemen (like Rainmaker Securities or structures similar to the bankrupt Linqto) collect fees for access to assets they frequently lack the legal right to transfer.

The fee structure is highly toxic: a typical multi-layered SPV charges a 2% management fee and a 20% success fee (carry) at every level. If an investor injects $2 million and the underlying asset grows to $10 million, nearly $5 million remains in the brokers' pockets before taxes.

Crucially, issuers (Anthropic, Anduril, OpenAI) strictly prohibit unauthorized secondary market transfers. Ultimate SPV investors do not physically own the underlying asset; they own a claim against a shell company. In the event of an IPO or liquidation, these transfers will be deemed void, redirecting all accumulated capital into years of litigation in the Delaware Chancery Court.

THE GLOBAL SOUTH'S AI MIGRATION

A tectonic shift is occurring in the tech sector across the Global South. American export controls and the paranoia surrounding model safety (e.g., the ban on Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos) have created a structural vacuum in Asian, African, and Latin American markets.

Because proprietary US AI models are becoming expensive, unreliable regarding access, and burdened with strict political censorship, developing nations are migrating en masse to Chinese open-weight models. Models like DeepSeek, Kimi, and Qwen are provided for free or at minimal cost. The Global South is completely unconcerned with Western narratives about "AI safety" or the ideological settings of the Chinese Communist Party embedded in these algorithms (censorship of topics like Taiwan is irrelevant to developers in Kenya or Brazil).

They view Chinese AI strictly as pragmatic, cheap, and uninterrupted technological infrastructure. By prohibiting tech exports, the US is voluntarily handing China a monopoly on the cognitive infrastructure for 80% of the planet's population.
