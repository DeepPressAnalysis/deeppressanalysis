EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The simultaneous escalation in the Middle East, featuring the collapse of the US-Iran truce, and an unexpected US diplomatic pivot at the NATO summit in Ankara serve as instruments for reconfiguring global markets and redirecting defense budgets. The US domestic political agenda is split between the managed elimination of an inconvenient radical-left Senate candidate, Graham Platner, and the aggressive, opaque expansion of Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), which is buying up tens of billions of dollars in commercial real estate amidst the extrajudicial killings of migrants. The media demonstrates deep polarization: some outlets normalize growing militarization and corporate windfall profits, while others mask systemic infrastructural and ethical crises as localized, isolated incidents, creating systemic blindness among the audience regarding the true beneficiaries of ongoing processes.
