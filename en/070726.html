THE NARRATIVE DIVIDE: EUPHORIA VS. PANIC

REPORT ON INFORMATION DISTORTIONS AND MARKET ARBITRAGE: JULY 2026

EUPHORIA ZONE (Over-hyped): The unimpeded expansion of artificial intelligence infrastructure and the imminent migration of mass users to decentralized social networks (SocialFi).

Main media thesis: "Global capital expenditures on AI infrastructure, fueled by the revolution in generative and agentic systems, will reach $750 billion in 2026, making hyperscalers invulnerable beneficiaries of the new economy, while decentralized social protocols (SocialFi) will permanently alter the landscape of digital ownership, forming multimillion-user active audiences free from corporate censorship."

PANIC ZONE (Over-hated): Traditional corporate software (SaaS), the private credit market financing it, as well as sectors of the "old industrial economy" and small-cap companies, demonized for their carbon footprint and technological backwardness.

Main media thesis: "The private credit market is on the verge of systemic collapse due to the rapid depreciation of traditional software assets amidst the AI revolution, while heavy industry and small-cap stocks are doomed to stagnation amid high interest rates and ESG pressure, forcing investors to liquidate positions en masse."

For each of these zones, we conducted a ruthless crash-test against reality.

02

CRASH-TESTING THE EUPHORIA ZONE

The Narrative: Capital is convinced that the only limitation to AI development is the speed of GPU production and the volume of venture funding. Institutional investors continue to pump liquidity into funds focused on building gigawatt-scale data centers, assuming hyperscaler demand automatically guarantees infrastructure supply. Simultaneously, in the Web3 segment, venture capital is aggressively selling the SocialFi narrative (Farcaster, Lens Protocol), convincing retail and institutional investors that the tokenization of social interactions and on-chain identity create sustainable economic value and ensure mass adoption of decentralized technologies in 2026.

Physical Reality: The narrative regarding exponential, seamless AI infrastructure growth entirely ignores the physical limits of power grids and harsh socio-political realities on the ground. According to a deep market audit, between January 2024 and May 2026, 46 major AI data center projects with a combined investment value exceeding $170 billion were blocked, frozen, or outright canceled in the US. The primary cause was not a semiconductor shortage, but fierce resistance from local communities (the NIMBY syndrome—Not In My Back Yard), outraged by the opacity of development contracts, the use of shell companies (LLCs) and non-disclosure agreements (NDAs), as well as the colossal consumption of freshwater and electricity.

The energy crisis has reached a stage of mathematical inevitability: PJM Interconnection, the largest regional grid operator in the US, projects a base capacity deficit of 6.6 gigawatts by 2027—a volume equivalent to the output of six to seven full-scale nuclear power plants. It is expected that by 2030, data centers will consume between 9.5% and 15.3% of all electricity generated in the US. As a result, hyperscalers are entering direct competition for base generation with heavy industry. Steel mills utilizing electric arc furnaces (consuming 40 to 200 MW each) face the prospect of sporadic blackouts, as utilities may be forced to prioritize data centers requiring uninterruptible power. Physical energy shortages and local political sabotage make the realization of the declared $750 billion in hyperscaler CapEx physically impossible within the timeframe designated by the market. Investors are paying for modeling projections that will shatter against the shortage of copper cable, transformers, and water usage permits.

In the SocialFi segment, physical reality is even more ruthless and cynical. Despite declarative claims of 8.2 million daily active wallets and hundreds of thousands of interactions, deep on-chain analytics reveal a catastrophic retention crisis. Studies show that 92% of SocialFi users permanently abandon platforms within 30 days of registration. Flagship protocols demonstrate illusory growth: Lens Protocol's daily active audience collapsed from a peak of 42,000 to a meager 8,000 users in just a few weeks, despite nominally having over 500,000 registered profiles.

The entire underlying economy of so-called "creator coins" operates as an algorithmic attention pyramid devoid of fundamental value. A prime example is the token of journalist Nick Shirley on the Base blockchain: fueled by virality in late 2025, the token reached a $9 million market capitalization, only to crash by 67% to $3 million by January 1, 2026. During this collapse, the creator extracted between $41,600 and $65,000 in fees in just two days. All activity in SocialFi boils down to retrodrop farming, Sybil attacks, and short-term speculation, rather than the formation of a sustainable social graph for which the market assigns premium venture valuations.

03

CRASH-TESTING THE PANIC ZONE

The Narrative: Institutional consensus has declared a "SaaS apocalypse." The implementation of generative AI and agentic systems supposedly renders traditional B2B software hopelessly obsolete. Consequently, the private credit market, which actively financed SaaS buyouts during the low-interest-rate era, is deemed a toxic asset. The financial press relishes data showing that the share of distressed private debt in the software sector, valued below 80% of par, reached a five-year high of 6.1% by the end of 2025. Public Business Development Companies (BDCs) are trading at a significant discount to Net Asset Value (NAV), and semi-liquid credit funds are facing a wave of redemption requests exceeding quarterly limits of 5%. Concurrently, the consensus buries old industrial sectors and small-cap stocks, viewing them as incapable of surviving in an era of high interest rates.

Physical Reality: The market fundamentally confuses valuation compression, caused by rising interest rates, with a fundamental collapse in operational business cash flows. Indeed, AI changes the development paradigm; however, AI integration at the level of the real corporate sector is proceeding much slower than newspaper hype suggests. As of late 2025, only 18% of US companies had integrated AI into actual production processes. Corporate software possesses colossal inertia, high switching costs, and strong vendor lock-in effects. Despite the decline in paper valuations of software assets by MSCI, the actual profit margins and cash interest coverage capacity remain remarkably robust in many cases. Private credit managers recorded high yields on underlying IT assets well into late 2025. Liquidity issues in BDCs and localized restructurings (such as the Tricolor and First Brands bankruptcies) have been illegitimately extrapolated to the entire $2 trillion asset class. The current discount to NAV in public credit structures is driven by liquidity panic and retail capital flight, not by a proportional rise in actual operational defaults on high-quality software.

In parallel, the ESG panic led to the closure of a record 91 funds in the US in 2025, with only 9 new ones launched. Capital is fleeing traditional industries due to regulatory and media pressure. However, the physical and economic reality is that this very "hated" heavy industry and small-cap stocks are extracting maximum benefit from the physical demands of AI and protectionist government policies. The Russell 2000 small-cap index ended the first half of 2026 with a 22% surge, posting its best performance since 1991 and vastly outperforming the overheated Nasdaq.

The fundamental anchor lies in the physical manifestation of infrastructure: the North American steel industry (giants like Nucor and Cleveland-Cliffs) is experiencing a renaissance. First, the renewal and doubling of Section 232 tariffs have securely insulated the US market from cheap imports, crashing foreign supply volumes by 52%. Second, every hyperscale data center requires up to 20,000 tons of structural steel. With an announced $50 billion in data center investments across North America, the steel sector is receiving unprecedented, guaranteed demand. While the media buries the old economy, its core components are quietly laying the foundation for the AI revolution, enjoying record price control and a complete lack of media scrutiny.

Gap Size: Critical.

ARBITRAGE STRATEGY: THE SHORT SIDE

An analysis of information distortions reveals structural inefficiencies in capital allocation. A pragmatic approach requires ignoring consensus and exploiting spreads between market valuation and the economic baseline.

A. THE SHORT SIDE (Candidates for Avoidance / Illusions)

What is at the peak of the media cycle but lacks the foundation to hold.

  • Asset/Sector: Data Center REITs in early project stages and AI infrastructure developers without guaranteed grid interconnection.
  • Why price/attention is inflated: The institutional market values these developers' portfolios solely on aggregated declared capacity metrics (megawatts/gigawatts), grossly ignoring local resistance and physical constraints. Valuations do not account for the fact that due to contract opacity (NDAs) and colossal water/energy depletion, local municipalities successfully block construction at the environmental permitting stage. Investors are paying an absurd premium for land that will never receive power from PJM or other operators under a projected macroeconomic deficit of 6.6 GW.
  • Resolution: Lock in profits on overheated infrastructure funds lacking physically connected facilities and active power purchase agreements. Short developers whose land banks (pipelines) are concentrated in jurisdictions with high civic activism and strict environmental regulations, where project cancellation risk is highest.
  • Asset/Sector: SocialFi protocols, decentralized social networks, and the creator economy (Creator Coins, Farcaster, Lens Protocol).
  • Why price/attention is inflated: Venture capitalists and retail traders evaluate the ecosystem solely on vanity metrics—"registered wallets" (over 2 million on Farcaster, over half a million on Lens), deliberately ignoring the fact that 92% of users vanish permanently after 30 days. The financial reward mechanics for early authors (creator coins) strictly incentivize bot farming and short-term liquidity extraction (pump-and-dump) rather than genuine creator migration from Web2. This is a classic metrics bubble funded by VC injections with zero signs of real product-market fit.
  • Resolution: Ignore the Web3 social networking narrative as a flawed investment thesis. Short venture valuations of SocialFi protocols at the earliest opportunity to open short positions on their liquid governance tokens, as the exhaustion of incentive programs (airdrop emissions) will trigger an immediate collapse in user activity.

WEB3 ASYMMETRY & REPRICING TRIGGERS

C. WEB3 / DECENTRALIZATION ASYMMETRY (Special Block)

Seeking arbitrage between the hype surrounding outdated centralized platforms and the real migration of value into decentralized protocols.

The Signal: While mainstream tech media and venture funds are obsessed with the idea of SocialFi as the next "Twitter killer," the real volume of Web3 infrastructure adoption is being generated in a completely different, far more pragmatic and cynical segment: cross-border payments, stablecoins, and global geopolitical sanctions evasion. Over the past year alone, sanctioned jurisdictions (Russia, Iran, North Korea) processed roughly $100 billion in cryptocurrency transactions to bypass the traditional banking system. Instruments like the A7A5 token (pegged to the ruble) allow local liquidity to be converted into global stablecoins (Tether) for international settlements. Concurrently, retail users in emerging economies (Vietnam, Nigeria, Turkey, Argentina), who constitute 62% of the active audience, utilize DeFi exclusively as a tool for surviving local hyperinflation and for cheap money transfers, rather than for governance token speculation.

The Distortion: Media and institutional consensus misjudge the growth drivers of decentralized solutions, focusing attention and capital on the "tokenization of social graphs" (the SocialFi illusion), while the true, pragmatic, and unshakeable Web3 user base is formed out of acute, indispensable financial survival necessity. The distortion lies in the fact that the infrastructure of stablecoins, tokenized Real World Assets (RWA), and decentralized payment gateways—which have become the systemic backbone of the shadow and emerging economy—trade at a discount compared to empty social networks. Redirecting institutional capital focus from "attention platforms" to "financial rails for grey and emerging markets" is the key arbitrage strategy in the crypto segment for the 2026-2027 horizon.

Mispricing can last for a long time. Here are 2-3 hard triggers that will force the media consensus to face reality and reprice assets:

  • AI Infrastructure & Energy: The gap will close when regional grid operators (primarily PJM Interconnection) officially publish reports of a critical base generation deficit of 6.6 GW and enforce legislative quotas or moratoriums on connecting new industrial facilities (data centers). This will force hyperscalers (Amazon, Microsoft, Google) to revise their CapEx projections sharply downward due to the physical impossibility of hosting or powering ordered equipment. Expected horizon: 3-6 months (the autumn-winter macroeconomic reporting cycle of energy operators).
  • Private Debt & BDCs: The gap will close when a series of quarterly reports from leading publicly traded BDCs demonstrates stable levels of real cash interest collection by portfolio SaaS companies, despite the drop in their nominal paper valuations. Steady dividend payouts by funds will force institutional yield-seeking investors to return to the sector, leading to an aggressive closing of the discount to NAV. Expected horizon: 6-9 months.
  • US Steel Industry: The gap will close when the next quarterly reports of Nucor and Cleveland-Cliffs directly reflect supply contracts for structural steel for data center construction (20,000 tons per facility), coinciding with confirmation that the US government will maintain 50% protective tariffs (Section 232). Expected horizon: 1-3 months.

Executive Summary: The market prices generative AI infrastructure as an infinitely scalable financial entity, blindly ignoring the physical laws of energy deficits and social resistance (NIMBY), while treating traditional SaaS servicing private credit debt and tariff-protected heavy industry as hopelessly obsolete and toxic assets. This creates a unique institutional opportunity for aggressive capital reallocation from overvalued data center developers and empty SocialFi ecosystems into the fundamental base of the real economy (the Russell 2000 index, metallurgy, SK hynix HBM memory) and undervalued BDC credit structures offering high discounts to NAV.
