The hidden motive for corporate top management's mass transition to private flights is the acute necessity to absolutely protect sensitive information from industrial cyber-espionage. For sophisticated investors, the business aviation infrastructure is becoming an extremely attractive asset class with high dividend yields and built-in inflation protection through dollarized tariffs. The used business jet market demonstrates striking price inefficiencies, offering hedge funds unique opportunities to extract risk-free arbitrage on the resale of scarce aircraft. The inevitable consolidation of the industry around three or four global players leads to rigid cartel pricing for mandatory maintenance, hangar storage, and aviation fuel. The use of business aviation has definitively ceased to be an attribute of demonstrative status, evolving into a rigid instrument for ensuring the physical safety of elites amidst global macroeconomic destabilization.
